We are a small, passionate group of weavers looking to build resources, teach and learn, and honour the rich tradition of basketry in Alberta.

Events

We hold three types of events during the year, open weaves, workshops, and our annual retreat "Weaving Connections”.

Open weaves do not have an instructor present and are free to attend. You can bring your own materials or coordinate with the host beforehand, and they can provide you with materials for a small gathering fee.

Workshops are led by experienced instructors, and materials are included in the price. Most workshops range between $65 (half day) to $250 (several days).

Weaving Connections is a 3-day retreat held in March at Aspen Ridge Greenhouse in Sherwood Park, workshops are offered throughout the weekend.

In the spirit of respect, reciprocity and truth the Alberta Basketry Guild honours and acknowledges that the land on which we gather to weave and share our craft is the traditional territory of many First Nations, Métis, and Inuit. We honour the territories of Treaty 4, 6, 7, 8, 10 and their signatories. We also acknowledge the homelands of the Otipemisiwak Métis Government, including the eight Métis Settlements and the 22 Métis Districts. 

We invite our members and participants to join us in the journey of learning, listening, reflecting, and engaging in actionable steps towards truth and reconciliation.
